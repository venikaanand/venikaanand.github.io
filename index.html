Redefining Cancer Treatment - MSKCC Kaggle Challenge

[Quick Guide]
Jan 2021 - Present
  • Problem Statement: The problem statement for the project has been inspired from the Kaggle challenge by MSKCC (Memorial Sloan Kettering Cancer Center). Every person has a unique combination of Gene and Variation which when cancerous can be mapped to a specific type of mutation. Gene is where the genetic mutation is located and variation is the amino acid for the mutation. Currently the process of mapping these unique set of Gene and Variation to a cancerous set of mutation is done through manual reading from a 70GB database of clinical research papers, which is a time taking process. If the process of detecting these mutations based on evidence from these research papers can be automated, this can aid the pathologists in speedy detection of cancerous mutations in genes
  • Approach: With our machine learning algorithm we classified the given genetic mutation/variation into 9 classes of cancerous mutations based on evidence collected from a text-based clinical literature (Research Papers)
  • Key Findings: Logistic Regression with bag of words vectorizer after optimal hyper paramter tuning was able to predict 77% of the patient records accurately, belonging to 9 classes of mutations
  • Impact: Interpretability was one of the core business requirement for the project. The results predicted were backed up by reasoning, which aid pathologists with faster detection of mutations with supporting theory from research papers
